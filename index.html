The Heartbeat of Melbourne’s Coffee Culture ☕

How time, drink choice, and place shape Melbourne's cafe habits

Melbourne's coffee culture is not just about what people drink. It follows a clear rhythm across the week, peaks sharply in the morning, and is shaped by where cafes are located and how much seating they provide. This visualisation follows that story from daily demand to neighbourhood-level cafe spaces.

Part 1

The Rhythm of Coffee Demand

The story begins with time. Melbourne's coffee demand is not random: it follows weekly routines, morning rush hours, and day-to-day fluctuations that reflect work, study, and commuting patterns.

Section 1 · Weekly pattern

Weekly Coffee Rhythms

Melbourne's coffee consumption follows a distinct weekly rhythm. Orders remain strong across weekdays, showing how much the city's coffee habit is connected to work and study routines. The weekend drop suggests that commuter traffic is a major driver of everyday cafe demand.

Section 2 · Morning rush

The Daily Caffeine Peak

The strongest signal appears when orders are grouped by time of day. Morning purchases dominate the dataset, matching the rush-hour window where people buy coffee before work, university, or daily activities.

Section 3 · Hour-by-hour history

Hourly Distribution of Sales

Looking more closely across the day shows exactly when cafes need to be ready for demand. The peak hour is annotated on the chart so the key number is visible immediately, rather than only being explained in the paragraph.

Section 4 · Daily trend

Daily Transaction Trends

Across the full timeline, daily totals rise and fall in repeated cycles. The annotated peak helps turn the line chart into a small story: demand changes day by day, but certain dates stand out as unusually busy.

Part 2

What Melbourne Chooses to Drink

After understanding when people buy coffee, the next question is what they choose. This section shifts from time patterns to product preference and spending behaviour.

Section 5 · Coffee popularity

Popularity of Beverage Types

The ranking highlights which drinks dominate Melbourne's cafe transactions. Direct labels on the bars make the most popular items easier to compare without forcing readers to estimate values from the axis.

Section 6 · Spending pattern

Transaction Value Distribution

The spending distribution shows the small but repeated purchases that define everyday cafe culture. Most transactions cluster around standard coffee prices, showing how frequent low-value sales can still form a major local economy.

Part 3

Where Coffee Culture Lives

The final part moves from transactions to place. It explores where cafes are located, how much seating they provide, and which neighbourhoods appear most concentrated.

Section 7 · Cafe map

Geographic Distribution

The map shows how cafes cluster across central Melbourne. The seating filter adds interactivity, allowing the viewer to compare indoor and outdoor cafe spaces rather than only seeing a static location plot.

Section 8 · Venue scale

Average Seating Capacity by Suburb

Seating capacity gives another way to understand cafe geography. Larger averages may suggest high-volume commercial districts, while smaller averages can indicate more compact neighbourhood or laneway-style cafe spaces.

Section 9 · Indoor vs outdoor

Indoor vs. Outdoor Seating Proportions

Melbourne's changing weather shapes how cafes organise their seating. Comparing indoor and outdoor capacity helps explain whether cafe spaces are designed more for year-round comfort or alfresco culture.

Section 10 · Neighbourhood density

Neighborhood Cafe Density

The final chart summarises the spatial story by ranking areas according to venue count. This shows where coffee culture is most concentrated and where competition between cafes is likely to be strongest.
