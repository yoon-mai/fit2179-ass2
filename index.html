The Heartbeat of Melbourne's Coffee Culture ☕

Exploring Melbourne's cafe culture through customer behaviour, menu trends, and business patterns
Melbourne is famous for its rich and vibrant coffee culture, where cafes are more than just places to grab a drink — they are part of everyday life. This visualisation explores the unique patterns behind Melbourne's cafe scene, from customer behaviour and popular drinks to cafe locations and business trends. By focusing on Melbourne first, we can better understand how coffee culture influences both social habits and the local cafe industry.

Melbourne in the Global Coffee Scene

Around the world, coffee is part of daily life — but in Australia, cafe culture has grown into something much larger. High sales volumes and strong customer spending place Australia among the world’s leading coffee markets. Within this landscape, Melbourne stands out for its dense cafe culture and unique customer habits, making it the perfect city to explore how coffee shapes both social behaviour and local business.

From this global view, we now zoom into Melbourne itself.

Daily Rhythms of Melbourne Coffee Orders

Melbourne's coffee culture follows a clear daily and weekly rhythm. These charts explore when demand is strongest and how customer ordering patterns shape everyday cafe activity.

Weekday demand consistently exceeds weekend activity, with Friday recording the highest order volume (285 orders). This pattern highlights the importance of workday routines in Melbourne's coffee culture.

The strongest demand occurs during weekday mornings, especially on Tuesday and Friday. Weekend orders are lower and less concentrated across specific time periods.

Orders increase rapidly during the morning and reach their highest point at 10:00 AM. Demand remains relatively strong throughout the afternoon, highlighting the role of coffee as a regular part of Melbourne's daily routine.

Neighbourhoods and Cafe Life in Melbourne

Melbourne's cafe culture extends far beyond the CBD. These charts examine how cafe activity, seating capacity, and neighbourhood characteristics vary across the city.

How Melbourne's Cafe Network Has Evolved

Cafe activity has expanded across Melbourne since 2002, with the CBD remaining the city's primary cafe hub. At the same time, surrounding neighbourhoods have gradually increased their presence, reflecting the broader growth of Melbourne's cafe culture.

Turning Coffee Data into Cafe Strategy

it is important to understand how customers typically spend. The following charts explore the factors that influence transaction value and purchasing behaviour.

Understanding Customer Spending

Most transactions fall within the $34-$36 range, suggesting a clear customer spending benchmark. This provides a useful reference point when designing pricing strategies and promotional offers.

Products with higher prices do not always have lower demand. Larger drinks and food items can still achieve strong sales, highlighting opportunities for cafes to grow revenue through value-based menu design.

Product Demand by Temperature

Demand is generally strongest during mild weather, with coffee orders peaking between 15°C and 20°C. Understanding these patterns can help cafes better anticipate demand and plan their product offerings.

Larger order sizes are most evident among older Australian customers, highlighting their importance to overall sales. While this customer segment appears highly engaged, cafes may be able to create additional growth opportunities by developing strategies that appeal more strongly to younger consumers.

Melbourne's cafe market is driven by predictable spending patterns, strong demand for higher-value products, and clear seasonal preferences. Businesses that align pricing, product offerings, and customer targeting strategies with these behaviours may be better positioned to increase sales.
